Mission

You weren’t crazy. The cloud got noisy.

RoachClaw Vault Maps Dev
Contained stack RoachNet

AI, maps, vault, and code kept close.

You used to sit down, open your laptop, and just build. Hours would disappear into code, writing, research — not into debugging your setup, chasing logins, or nursing along some “smart” cloud tool that keeps trying to help by getting in your way.

Now your work lives in a dozen tabs, three identity providers, and however many SaaS dashboards decided to redesign themselves this quarter. Your “stack” feels less like a system and more like a pile.

And yet, for the work that actually matters, you still crave something simple:

That’s where RoachNet comes in.

A machine that’s yours. Tools that don’t move. A setup that keeps working, even when the internet doesn’t.

Meet RoachNet — an offline-first suite for AI, archiving, and development that actually lives on your machines.

RoachNet treats your computer like a computer again. It’s a self-contained environment where your models, your tools, and your data run locally, sync on your terms, and don’t vanish when a login expires or a product manager gets bored.

Instead of asking “which service holds this?”, you always know: it’s in RoachNet.

Spin up local AI with RoachClaw. Keep a durable archive of the web, docs, and projects you care about. Ship code from a reproducible dev environment that’s the same on your desktop, your laptop, or your lab machine.

No surprise upgrades. No forced migrations. No “we’ve updated our terms” emails between you and your work.

Once you stop assuming everything has to be hosted elsewhere, your whole relationship with computing shifts. You start:

  • Keeping your core tools close, instead of chasing new dashboards.
  • Treating the internet as an optional bonus, not a dependency.
  • Designing workflows that can’t be turned off from someone else’s control panel.

RoachNet is built for that mindset. For people who like to own the stack they rely on, even if they still use plenty of cloud on top.

It’s not nostalgia. It’s just a quieter, saner foundation for modern work — one where your AI, your archive, and your dev tools are portable, offline-capable, and under your control.

You can keep clicking through other people’s platforms. Or you can bring the important stuff home.
